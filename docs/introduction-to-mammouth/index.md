# Get Started

## What is Mammouth?

Mammouth is a platform that gives you access to the best AI models on the market in a single interface.

Thanks to the **reprompting feature** and **custom mammouths**, you can compare model responses and improve your daily productivity.

## 💬 Ask Your First Question

To ask a question on Mammouth, simply type your message in the **conversation bar**, then **select the model** you want to query.

Models compatible with your prompt appear automatically.

<img src="/docs/introduction-to-mammouth/1st_question_12s.gif" alt="1st_question.gif" style="height: auto;" />

## 🔀 Switch Default Models

The first model on the left is selected by default. 

Drag and drop the models to the left to change your favorite. Click on the icons to view all available models.

<img src="/docs/introduction-to-mammouth/default_model_12s.gif" alt="default_model.gif" style="height: auto;" />

## 🔄 Compare the Response with Another Model

Mammouth allows you to redirect a question to another model to compare/complement the response, without needing to rework the prompt or copy the text into another conversation.

This is the **reprompting feature**.

👉 **More** : if you reprompt a later message in the conversation, the whole history will be sent to the new model

### The Importance of Careful Writing

The quality and relevance of the visual result directly depend on the precision of your wording.

<div class="failed-box">
     <span class="icon">🚫</span>
<div class="content">
    Spelling errors or confusing wording can mislead the artificial intelligence and generate an image far from your initial vision.
</div>
    </div>    
    <div class="success-box">
    <span class="icon">✅</span>
        <div class="content">
            To ensure the effectiveness of your instruction, three elements are essential: careful spelling, clear syntax, and coherent organization.
        </div>
</div>

Optimization also involves successive adjustments:
- switching to English,
- eliminating superfluous wording,
- structuring with bullet points...

Each AI model has its own specificities. Nevertheless, a methodical approach based on a few key questions allows you to structure your thinking and develop an instruction that is simultaneously explicit, precise, and relevant.

### Describing Your Vision in a Structured Way

<div class="info-box">
    <span class="icon">⬇️</span>
<div class="content">An effective prompt is based on five key steps:
        <ol>
        <li>
            <strong>Major Components</strong> - Clearly identify who or what constitutes the heart of the image
        </li>
        <li>
            <strong>Visual Styles and Artistic Inspirations</strong> - Specify the desired aesthetic: movement, era, or artistic reference
        </li>
        <li>
        <strong>Lighting and Effects</strong> - Define the light atmosphere and textures to bring the scene to life
        </li>
            <li>
                <strong>Angles and Perspectives</strong> - Choose the optimal viewpoint to showcase your subject
            </li>
            <li>
                <strong>Image Format</strong> - Specify the appropriate dimensions and proportions
            </li>
        </ol>
    <p class="conclusion">
        This hierarchical structure ensures consistent results that conform to your creative vision.
    </p>
</div>
</div>

#### Major Components

- **Characters:** gender, age, emotion, position
- **Objects:** distinctive accessories, decorative elements
- **Scene:** precise setting (jungle, cozy living room, Parisian street)
- **Movement:** still or in action?

#### **Visual Styles and Inspirations**

Photography

- **Realistic**: detailed photography, high definition
- **Black and White**: pronounced contrast, dramatic lighting

Classical Paintings

- **Watercolor**: fluid colors, transparency
- **Impressionism**: fragmented brushstrokes, light capture (Monet)
- **Van Gogh**: swirling whorls, generous impasto
- **Surrealism**: dreamlike, distorted reality, unbridled imagination (Dalí)
- **Cubism**: spatial deconstruction, multiple perspectives (Picasso)

Decorative Arts

- **Gothic**: mysterious darkness, monumental architecture
- **Baroque**: sumptuous ornaments, gilding, theatricality
- **Art Nouveau**: organic curves, Mucha floral motifs
- **Art Deco**: stylized geometry, 1920s elegance

Graphic Arts

- **Minimalist**: refined composition, elegant sobriety
- **Pop Art**: saturated colors, comic book codes, Warhol style
- **Street Art**: urban expression, spray paint, Banksy style

Animation

- **Anime/Manga**: expressive lines, vivid colors, Japanese style
- **Studio Ghibli**: visual poetry, soft light, dreamlike atmosphere
- **Pixel Art**: retro-gaming aesthetic, 8-bit rendering

Imaginary Worlds

- **Cyberpunk**: futuristic, bright neons, technological dystopia
- **Steampunk**: Victorian-tech, copper gears, steam era
- **Fantasy**: imaginary universes, legendary creatures

#### **Lighting and Effects**

- **Lighting:** natural, dramatic, cinematic
- **Warm tones**: red/orange/yellow (energy)
- **Cool tones**: blue/green/purple (calm)
- **Pastel**: muted softness (lightness)
- **Effects and textures**: contrast, saturation, grain, artistic blur

#### Angles and Perspective

- **Close-up**: detailed facial expressions
- **Aerial view**: panorama, overall staging
- **Fish-eye**: immersive effect, dynamic distortion
- **Wide shot**: extended scene, ideal for landscapes/environments
- **Low angle**: grandeur, power, view from below
- **Backlight**: silhouettes, cinematic effect

#### Image Format

- **Orientation**: portrait, landscape
- **Ratio: `--ar 1:1` (square)**, **`--ar 2:3`**, **`--ar 3:2`**, **`--ar 4:5`**, **`--ar 5:4`**, **`--ar 4:3`**, **`--ar 3:4`**, **`--ar 16:9`** or **`--ar 9:16`**

<div class="warning-box">
    <span class="icon">⚠</span>
<div class="content"><strong>Pitfalls to Avoid:</strong>
    <ul>
        <li>
                <strong>Information overload</strong>: too much information overwhelms the AI and generates incoherent images
        </li>
        <li>
            <strong>Lack of precision</strong>: a vague prompt produces unpredictable and random results
        </li>
        <li>
            <strong>Contradictory instructions</strong>: the AI cannot resolve conflicting directives
        </li>
        <li>
            <strong>Poor prioritization</strong>: always place essential elements at the beginning of the prompt
        </li>
    </ul>
</div>
</div>

### Improvement Through Iteration and Reprompting

After writing your prompt, test it and adjust it based on the results.

3 tips to become a pro at image generation:

<div class="tip-box">
    <span class="icon">🥉</span>
    <div class="content">
        <strong>Modify one parameter at a time</strong> to progressively refine the generated image.
    </div>
</div>

<div class="tip-box">
    <span class="icon">🥈</span>
    <div class="content" >
        <strong>Ask the AI to refine your prompt</strong> for greater clarity and fluidity.<br> <br>
        <em>This approach can result in a particularly powerful custom mammouth.</em>
    </div>
</div>

<div class="tip-box">
    <span class="icon">🥈</span>
    <div class="content" >
        <strong>Reprompt with another model!</strong> <br> <br>
        41% of image queries are reprompted by our users (vs 34% for text). <br>
        <strong>Why?</strong> Images are inherently subjective: visual style, composition, and artistic interpretation vary greatly from one model to another. Comparison becomes almost indispensable.
    </div>
</div>
